Monkeys: Intelligent, social primates found in tropical forests and savannas, known for their agility, curiosity, and ability to use tools in some species.

Geological Nature

Refers to the Earth’s physical structure, including its landforms, oceans, atmosphere, and hydrosphere. This type of nature encompasses the processes that shape the planet, such as volcanic activity, tectonic movements, and weathering.

Biological Nature

Includes all living organisms, from bacteria to plants and animals, as well as their interactions with each other and their environment. This type of nature encompasses ecosystems, such as forests, grasslands, deserts, tundra, and freshwater and marine environments.

Oceanic Nature

Specifically refers to the world’s oceans, including their chemistry, physics, and biology. This type of nature is characterized by vast expanses of water, diverse marine life, and complex interactions between ocean currents, waves, and tides.

Atmospheric Nature

Encompasses the Earth’s atmosphere, including its composition, temperature, humidity, and circulation patterns. This type of nature affects global climate, weather patterns, and the distribution of life on Earth.

Philosophical Nature

Represents humanity’s understanding and perception of nature, including cultural, spiritual, and aesthetic values. This type of nature encompasses the ways in which humans relate to and interact with the natural world, including concepts such as wilderness, conservation, and sustainability.
